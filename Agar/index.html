APGAR Scoring System

Newborn Health Assessment

A quick, standardized method used worldwide to assess a newborn’s condition immediately after birth.

When is it done?

  • 1 minute after birth: Evaluates how well the baby tolerated the birthing process.
  • 5 minutes after birth: Assesses how well the baby is adapting to the new environment.
  • If the score is low, assessments may be repeated every 5 minutes.

What APGAR stands for

A - Appearance (Skin Color)

0 → Blue/pale all over
1 → Pink body, blue extremities
2 → Fully pink

Checks oxygenation and blood circulation through skin color.

P - Pulse (Heart Rate)

0 → No heartbeat
1 → <100 bpm
2 → >100 bpm

Assesses heart function and circulatory stability.

G - Grimace (Reflex)

0 → No response
1 → Weak
2 → Strong reaction

Evaluates reflexes and nervous system responsiveness.

A - Activity (Muscle Tone)

0 → Limp
1 → Some movement
2 → Active movement

Checks muscle tone and neurological activity.

R - Respiration (Breathing Effort)

0 → None
1 → Weak
2 → Strong cry

Assesses breathing effectiveness and lung function.

Total Score Interpretation

7–10 → Normal
4–6 → Moderate distress
0–3 → Severe distress

Important Clinical Notes

  • The APGAR score is a rapid screening tool, not a diagnosis.
  • A low score does not necessarily mean long-term problems.
  • It helps guide immediate medical care decisions.
  • Various factors (like prematurity) can influence the score.
